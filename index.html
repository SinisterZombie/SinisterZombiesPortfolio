About Me

Hi, I’m SinisterZombie, a Roblox developer who loves making fun and nostalgic games. I’ve been playing Roblox for over 10 years, using Roblox Studio for 7+ years, and coding in Lua for 2+ years. I also have 1 year of experience making music for games.

My Projects:

- Pizza Factory Tycoon
- The Horror Elevator
- The Scary Elevator

Portfolio

Roblox 2016 UI

This project is a recreation of the classic Roblox UI from 2016. It features the old features such as old UI, drop hats, and more. This project is a tribute to the old Roblox UI and the nostalgia it brings to players.

Game Recreate

A recreation of classic Roblox games that were popular in the past. These projects aim to bring back the old version of the game and nostalgia of old Roblox games for players to enjoy them once again.

Pizza Factory Tycoon

This is one of my most popular recreation games! This game originally create by UltraW, known for creating many tycoon game such as "Restaurant Tycoon". In this game, you get to build and manage your own pizza factory. Start by making pizzas, earning money, and growing your business. Unlock new types of pizzas as you progress and create the ultimate pizza restaurant to serve your customers.

Ultimate Driving

This is the most complex game I’ve ever recreated! Originally made by TwentyTwoPilots, this game is all about role-playing and choosing from many jobs, like Police Officer, Firefighter, Truck Driver, Highway Worker, or even just being a regular citizen. You can earn money by driving around and use it to buy new cars. Or own a house and doing everying in this open-world driving game!

The Horror Elevator

This game was originally created by zMadZeus, but it was removed from Roblox due to a copyright strike. In this game, you find yourself trapped in a Horror Elevator. Each time the elevator stops at a new floor, you’ll face a terrifying ghost or killer trying to hunt you down. Your goal is to survive as long as you can while the horrors keep coming!

Rob a Bank Obby

This game was originally created by PlatinumFalls, a legendary obby creator everyone knows. In this game, your mission is to rob a bank and escape as fast as you can—but it’s not as easy as it sounds! Each stage is filled with tricky obstacles and puzzles (or what we call an obby). Once you successfully rob the bank, you’ll make your escape like an EPIC players!

The Scary Elevator

This game was originally created by MrNotSoHERO. The gameplay is similar to The Horror Elevator—you survive floors filled with scares and earn points for each floor you make it through. You can then use those points to buy cool stuff. This recreation is listed last because I didn’t do as much for it; my main work was fixing and improving the scripts to make the game run smoother.

My backstory

On January 26, 2010, I joined the world (I mean born, but it sounds cooler this way XD). Growing up in Thailand, I lived at my uncle’s house, which was also an internet cafe. When I was around 3 years old, I got to use a computer for the first time. At first, I spent most of my time watching YouTube, but then my dad introduced me to games like Ragnarok Online, Minecraft, and Plants vs. Zombies.

Then, in 2014, when I was 4 years old, I discovered an amazing game called Roblox on YouTube. I asked my dad to help me sign up, and I instantly fell in love with it. I even begged him to buy me Roblox shirts! A few years later, around 2016 or 2017, I found out that you could actually create games on Roblox. Before that, I thought Roblox made all the games themselves!

I didn’t seriously start creating games until 2021, when I was 11 years old. My friends and I wanted to build our own city in Roblox, and that’s where I learned a lot of skills. Then, in 2022, I created my first big project: a Roblox version of my school as part of a school project. That was the real start of my game development journey.

After that, I recreated the old Roblox UI, which became super popular. That’s when Sonicmaven, a fellow developer, reached out to me. He loved my work and offered to help make my game more popular. I accepted, and thanks to his help, my game blew up! I even earned a lot of Robux, which was a dream come true for me as a kid.

I’m so grateful for everything that has happened and everyone who has helped me become the developer I am today. :)
